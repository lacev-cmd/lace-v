Mercator module

A private map for what is actually happening.

Mercator is the current working surface. It uses domain cartridges to shape the language, gaps, pressure signals and examples while the core engine remains domain-agnostic.

01

Choose a map.

Life, job hunt, business, project, decision, household, relationship, rehab, inside, reentry and more.

02

Set the baseline.

The honest starting picture: current state, constraints, pressure, direction, and what is being avoided.

03

Add entries.

The map reads material over time, not one polished story. Movement and non-movement need dated material.

04

Correct the read.

If the map is wrong, the correction becomes part of future interpretation rather than cosmetic feedback.

05

Generate report.

The report shows movement, load, gaps, tensions, avoidance, capability signals and the next useful move.

06

Export or close.

The standalone can print/save reports and export local data. It does not send material anywhere.

Privacy and boundary

Private first. Claims controlled.

This standalone version is for local testing. It stores data in the browser and does not send data anywhere. Distribution versions need the PIN/passphrase layer before pilot use.

Not therapy. No diagnosis, treatment, clinical support, or professional replacement.
Not advice. The map shows what the material supports; the decisions remain with the user.
Not surveillance. The private map is not a reporting-back or compliance-monitoring surface.
Not a risk score. The system does not score people, predict behaviour, or make legal findings.
Prototype storage. Browser-only local storage. Not encrypted yet. Clearing browser data can erase entries.
Distribution requirement. PIN/passphrase encryption comes before any serious pilot or distribution conversation.
