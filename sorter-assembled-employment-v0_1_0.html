Employment Map

Job searching is harder than it looks from the outside and lonelier than most people admit. This tool doesn't offer career advice or tell you what to apply for. It reads what you write and maps what's actually happening — what activity is producing results, what isn't, where the pressure is coming from, what the gaps are.

Write what's happening. The map does the rest.

HOW TO USE THIS TOOL

Write where the search stands — what you're going for, what you've tried, what's come back, what the financial pressure looks like. Come back when something moves. The map reads across all of it.

A minimum baseline might look like this:
"I've been out of work for two months. I'm applying but not hearing back. I don't know if it's my CV, the roles I'm going for, or just the market. The money situation is starting to affect how clearly I can think about it."

Write more if you have it. That's enough if you don't.

This tool is not a substitute for professional support. It reads what you write and maps what it finds — no more. It does not diagnose, assess risk, or produce a clinical or legal finding. If you are in crisis or need immediate help, please reach out to someone who can support you directly.
